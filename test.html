Sinbound Rulebook (First Edition)

1. Game Overview

Sinbound is a 1–4 player strategic card game set in the gothic world of the Hellskein, where players wield power through ancient sins, twisted thralls, and broken pacts. The game revolves around managing different types of Sin as resources while reducing the opponent’s Leader to 0 HP.

2. Objective

Reduce your opponent’s Leader to 0 HP.

3. Setup

  • Each Leader starts on the battlefield with 50 HP in a Dormant state.
  • Each player builds a deck of exactly 60 cards.
  • Decks may optionally include a Sin Pact.
  • Each player draws 5 cards to start.
  • Each player chooses a Leader, which begins Dormant.
  • Randomly determine who goes first.

4. Card Types

  • Thralls – Minions used to attack, defend, and generate Sin.
  • Spells – One-time effects that change the game state.
  • Rituals – Expensive, powerful effects paid for with Sin.
  • Relics – Permanent cards that grant effects.
  • Leaders – Begin on the battlefield Dormant. Each has HP, ATK, and a passive ability.

5. Resources

Sin is the game’s core resource. There are 7 types: Greed, Wrath, Lust, Envy, Sloth, Gluttony, and Pride, plus Generic Sin.

Sin Engines:

  • At the start of each turn, each player creates 1 Generic Sin Engine.
  • Each Sin Engine generates 1 Sin of its type at the start of its controller's turn.

6. Turn Structure

Start of Turn

  • Create 1 Generic Sin Engine.
  • All Sin Engines generate Sin.

Main Phase

  • Play cards by paying their Sin costs.
  • Activate abilities.
  • Bind cards.
  • Attack with Thralls (Thralls cannot attack the turn they enter unless stated).

End Phase

  • Discard down to 7 cards.
  • Resolve end of turn effects.

7. Combat

  • Declare attackers.
  • Opponent declares blockers.
  • Damage is dealt simultaneously.
  • Thralls with 0 HP are discarded.

8. Keywords & Terms

  • Bind – Turn a card sideways to show it's used.
  • Abyss / Abyssed – Cards removed from the game.
  • Sinbound – Tied to one of the Seven Sins.
  • Sin Engine – Generates Sin each turn.
  • Pact – Deckbuilding restriction with a benefit.
  • Generic Sin – Can be used as any Sin, but not for Sin-specific effects.
  • Dormant – Leaders start in this state. Cannot attack, block, be targeted, or use passives until awakened.

9. Sin Pacts (Optional)

During deckbuilding, players may declare a Sin Pact:

  • Restriction: Example: Only use cards tied to Gluttony.
  • Boon: Example: Start with an extra Sin Engine or draw effect.

10. Victory & Defeat

  • Lose when your Leader reaches 0 HP.
  • Lose if you try to draw from an empty deck.

11. Additional Mechanics

These will appear in future sets:

  • Corruption – Gradual transformation of cards.
  • Multi-Sin Rituals – Expensive and powerful effects.
  • Sinbound Species – Lore-based mechanics (e.g., angels, fleshweavers).

Appendix A – Sin Types

  • Greed – Hoarding, gold, dominion.
  • Wrath – Flame, fury, annihilation.
  • Lust – Desire, illusion, temptation.
  • Envy – Theft, mimicry, subversion.
  • Sloth – Delay, inevitability, rot.
  • Gluttony – Hunger, waste, bloat.
  • Pride – Power, control, reflection.
