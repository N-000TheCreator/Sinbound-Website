Sinbound Lore

Chapter I: The One Who Watched

Before there was Sin, there was the Serathiel. He was not born but assigned, not named but numbered. N.-000, the Seamless One. His was the burden of observation, not judgment, and he carried it in silence across eons.

Chapter II: The Final Hour

N.999, the Final Hour, came not to act but to unmake. It watched time decay, and saw the Seamless One not act. When entropy touched will, the two numbers clashed — not in violence, but in paradox. Their battle left fractures in the firmament.

Chapter III: The Unbound Flame

A wanderer rose from ash and oathbreakers — immune to Sin, bound to none. They walk the Hellskein still. Their presence stirs the Sins, and the Seamless One watches once more.

Chapter IV: The Sinbound Thirteen

Thirteen figures claimed the power of Sin, each reshaping a domain within the Hellskein. They made pacts with mortals, bred Thralls, and turned engines of guilt into fuel. The Accord holds, but barely.

Chapter V: The Hollow Angel

They say Serathiel’s remnants dream. Deep in the Pale Hollow, a pulse grows louder. Some call it hope. Others, relapse.

Chapter VI: The Seamless One Stirs

It is said that the Seamless One was once Serathiel. Or perhaps Serathiel was only a mask. Either way, its six arms have begun to move.

Species in Hell

Hollowborn

Hollowborns are the beings of Guilt. Appearing wherever one feels shamed.

Fleshweavers

These flesh-crafters of Gluttony shape matter by hunger and mutation. They fashion living weapons, bloated horrors, and a god that will cleanse all of Hell.

Ashwraiths

Ashwraiths are the souls of those who were wronged in life. When a spirit cannot let go of its pain, it ignites into flame, drifting through Hell as a smoldering ghost.

Brimstone Titans

Brimstone Titans are the oldest living things in Hell. Formed when the plane was still molten, they are walking mountains, crusted with obsidian and magma.

Chitterfiends

Chitterfiends weren’t supposed to exist. They emerged within Scythemite broods, smaller, with faces like carved bone and limbs serrated like meat hooks.

Gravewings

They began as simple carrion beasts, but over time, exposure to cursed winds and the ash of ancient spells awakened a terrible intellect in them.

Ironborns

The Ironborn were not born of Hell — they were made in it. Beings captured were twisted into metal-bodied war machines, fused with blades, armor, and engines of death.

Misery Imps

The Misery Imps were born from the first regrets ever felt in Hell — little ripples of despair that splintered off from damned souls and took shape.

Scornbeasts

Bred in the war pits of Hell’s blood arenas, Scornbeasts were once bestial gladiators. But over centuries of bloodshed, they learned to humiliate before the kill.

Scythemites

The Scythemites are towering insectoid reapers charged with enforcing Hell's laws.

Tariss

Native to the slick obsidian tunnels far below Hell’s molten crust, the Tariss are small hunters. But what they lack in size, they make up for in overwhelming numbers, hyper-coordination, and ambush instincts.

Wyrmreapers

The Wyrmreapers were among the first to crawl through Hell. Ancient dragon-centipede hybrids, they were born from the eruption that first birthed the realm.

Ashenborn

Ashenborn are made of smoke, shadow, and memory. They came into existence during the first betrayals in Hell.
